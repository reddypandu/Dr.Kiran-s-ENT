Foreign Body removal ear,nose & throat

Foreign body removal from the ear, nose, or throat is a common procedure performed by otolaryngologists (ENT specialists) to extract objects that have become lodged in these areas. Foreign bodies can include items such as beads, insects, food particles, small toys, or pieces of jewelry. In the ear, foreign bodies can cause pain, discomfort, hearing loss, or even damage to the ear canal or eardrum if not promptly removed. In the nose, foreign bodies may lead to nasal congestion, bleeding, or infection. In the throat, they can cause difficulty swallowing, coughing, or choking. Foreign body removal in the ear, nose, and throat (ENT) involves extracting objects that have become lodged in these anatomical areas. In the throat, fish bones, food particles, or other objects can cause discomfort or potential complications if not removed promptly. Removal techniques vary depending on the location and nature of the foreign body and may include gentle suction, forceps, irrigation, or specialized tools. Prompt removal is crucial to prevent complications such as infection, inflammation, or damage to surrounding tissues. In some cases, sedation or anesthesia may be necessary, especially for young children or if the object is deeply lodged.
